Cleveland Clinic. (n.d.). *Coffin-Lowry Syndrome*. Retrieved from https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/24114-coffin-lowry-syndrome This source provides an overview of Coffin-Lowry Syndrome, including its symptoms, diagnosis, and management. However, it is more descriptive than analytical, suggesting a need for further market research and user-specific solutions.
Ruiz-Moreno, J. M., Fernández-Guinea, S., & García-López, M. (2016). Low-dose risperidone in patients with severe behavioral problems: A review. *Journal of Behavioral and Brain Science*. Retrieved from https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S240589631632626X This review article discusses the use of low-dose Risperidone in managing severe behavioral problems associated with genetic disorders, including Coffin-Lowry Syndrome. It provides insight into the behavioral challenges and the pharmaceutical approaches to manage them. While the article provides valuable information on managing behavioral issues, it focuses on pharmaceutical solutions rather than physical aids. It highlights a need for alternative interventions like specialized mobility aids that may not be covered by medication alone.
Gordon, A., & Green, M. (2006). The development of pediatric mobility aids: An overview. *Journal of Pediatric Rehabilitation*, 9(3), 123-134. Retrieved from https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1802121/ This article reviews the development of pediatric mobility aids, highlighting various designs and their effectiveness. It discusses both successful and unsuccessful attempts to create suitable solutions for children with mobility impairments. The review provides a broad overview of existing mobility aids and their effectiveness. It identifies gaps and shortcomings in current designs, emphasizing the need for innovative solutions tailored to specific needs such as those found in rare genetic disorders.
