3D Prototype Design

1. I started my 3D prototype by designing a 5 by 2 inch rectangle and then extruding it to a height of 1.75 inches, resulting in a rectangular prism. This prism would serve as my raw material, allowing me to chisel away unnecessary parts and craft my masterpiece like a sculptor.

2. Next, I extruded into the side of the rectangle by -0.6 inches to carve out space for attaching the wheels, gradually transforming the large rectangle into a model car.

3. Third, I sketched two identical circles on the same side of the rectangle where I made the previous extrusion. Each circle has a diameter of 1.3 inches and is evenly spaced to enhance the carâ€™s design. I then selected only the upper halves of the circles and extruded them by -0.6 inches, creating the wheel arches for the model car.
